# content_warnings — Proposals

Tracks proposed additions to the `content_warnings` strictly controlled vocabulary.

**Important:** This is a strictly controlled type. New additions require a GitHub Issue with:
- Clear, one-sentence definition
- Distinguishing rule from existing warnings
- At least 5 example works where this warning applies
- Explicit maintainer sign-off before a PR will be accepted

See [AGENTS.md](../AGENTS.md) and [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md).

## Pending

### Miscarriage / Pregnancy Loss
- **Proposed:** 2026-04 (initial)
- **Definition:** Depiction of the loss of a pregnancy — miscarriage, stillbirth, or termination in a grief context — with sufficient emotional weight to be distressing to readers who have experienced pregnancy loss.
- **Distinguishing rule:** The Substantiality Rule applies: a brief, clinical mention does not warrant a warning. A sustained depiction of a character's grief, physical experience, or relationship fallout from pregnancy loss does. Distinct from `Child death` (which is about a child who has lived) and from reproductive topics covered as `main_topics`.
- **Example works:** *The Light We Lost* (Santopolo), *How to Stop Time* (Haig — brief mention, may not warrant), *Little Fires Everywhere* (Ng)
- **Notes:** Strong candidate. Readers who have experienced pregnancy loss frequently report being blindsided by these depictions in fiction. The experience is distinct enough from general `Child death` to warrant its own warning.

### Medical Trauma
- **Proposed:** 2026-04 (initial)
- **Definition:** Sustained depiction of distressing medical procedures, hospitalization, chronic illness, or the experience of navigating medical systems in ways that may be triggering for readers with medical trauma or health anxiety.
- **Distinguishing rule:** The Substantiality Rule applies. A character catching the flu is not medical trauma. Extended depictions of surgery, ICU stays, terminal diagnosis, or medical gaslighting may be. Distinct from `Terminal illness` (proposed separately) in that Medical Trauma focuses on the *experience of care*, not the outcome.
- **Example works:** *The Fault in Our Stars* (Green), *A Little Life* (Yanagihara — multiple), *The Year of Magical Thinking* (Didion — grief and medical)
- **Notes:** Moderate candidate. The definition needs to be tighter to distinguish from everyday illness depictions. "Medical trauma" as a psychological category is real and recognized; the challenge is defining the threshold.

### Antisemitism / Religious hate
- **Proposed:** 2026-04 (initial)
- **Definition:** Significant depiction of antisemitic attitudes, rhetoric, violence, or persecution; or hate directed at other religious groups as a primary form of discrimination.
- **Distinguishing rule:** The existing `Racism / Racial slurs` warning is intended for racial discrimination; antisemitism and religious discrimination are distinct categories with distinct histories that may not be captured by a racial framing. This warning applies when the discrimination is directed at religious identity rather than (or in addition to) racial identity.
- **Example works:** *Maus* (Spiegelman), *Night* (Wiesel), *The Diary of a Young Girl* (Frank), *The Merchant of Venice* (Shakespeare)
- **Notes:** Moderate candidate. The existing Racism warning may be sufficient if interpreted broadly. The question is whether readers searching for a specific content warning about antisemitism would find "Racism / Racial slurs" adequate. Recommendation: accept if there is community consensus that the existing warning is insufficient.

### Stalking / Harassment
- **Proposed:** 2026-04 (initial)
- **Definition:** Depiction of sustained unwanted pursuit, surveillance, or harassment of a person by another — whether framed as romantic, threatening, or ambiguous.
- **Distinguishing rule:** Distinct from `Domestic violence` (which is within an established relationship) and `Abuse (emotional)` (which may not involve pursuit). Stalking and harassment can occur between strangers or acquaintances and the definition of "unwanted" is central. Note: some classic Romances frame stalking behavior positively — these should still receive the warning if the behavior objectively fits the definition, regardless of the framing.
- **Example works:** *You* (Kepnes), *The Talented Mr. Ripley* (Highsmith), various Gothic Romances
- **Notes:** Strong candidate. Stalking is a distinct experience for readers who have been stalked, and the behavior appears in many Romances and Thrillers with varying framing.

### Terminal Illness
- **Proposed:** 2026-04 (initial)
- **Definition:** A character's experience of a terminal diagnosis and the process of dying from illness is a sustained, central element of the narrative.
- **Distinguishing rule:** The Substantiality Rule applies: a character who dies of illness off-page is not sufficient. The warning applies when the experience of illness — the diagnosis, treatment, decline, and death — is depicted on the page and is central to the story. Distinct from `Medical Trauma` (which is about the experience of care) and `Child death` (about who dies).
- **Example works:** *The Fault in Our Stars* (Green), *Me Before You* (Moyes), *When Breath Becomes Air* (Kalanithi)
- **Notes:** Strong candidate. Many readers specifically seek or avoid illness narratives. The category is distinct and meaningful.

## Deferred

### "Pregnancy" (general)
- **Proposed:** (common request)
- **Decision:** Deferred — 2026-04
- **Reasoning:** Pregnancy alone is not a content warning — it is a life event that appears in countless works without being distressing. The warning is warranted for specific distressing experiences around pregnancy (loss, unwanted pregnancy, medical complications), not pregnancy itself. `Miscarriage / Pregnancy loss` (above) captures the distressing case. Revisit if a better-scoped definition can be found.

### "Religious content"
- **Proposed:** (common request)
- **Decision:** Deferred — 2026-04
- **Reasoning:** Religion appears in books in too many ways to warrant a blanket warning. A warning implies potential harm, and religious content per se is not harmful. `main_topics:Religion` handles subject coverage. If the concern is about anti-religious content or religious trauma narratives, a more specific warning (Religious trauma, Cult dynamics) may be warranted. Revisit with a tighter scope.
