# sub_topics

**Definition:** The specific, secondary elements that populate the world of a work — supporting subjects that are present and meaningful but do not drive the central premise.

---

## What is a Sub Topic?

A sub topic is the world-building material and supporting subject matter of a work. It is what the world *contains* without being what the story is *about*.

If `main_topics` answer "what is this book dealing with?", `sub_topics` answer "what else is noticeably present in this book's world?"

---

## Core Rules

**Do use sub_topics to capture:**
- Secondary subjects that recur meaningfully but aren't the central concern
- The texture of the world: occupations, customs, flora, fauna, institutions, subcultures
- Background elements that a knowledgeable reader would notice and value finding in a search

**Do NOT put in sub_topics:**
- The central thesis or subject (that's `main_topics`)
- Physical objects used as narrative props (that's `things`)
- Settings and locations (that's `places`)
- Characters and people (that's `people`)
- Abstract meanings and ideas (that's `literary_themes`)

**The Background Test:** If you're describing the world of the book to someone who hasn't read it, sub_topics are the supporting details you'd mention after covering the main point. "It's a novel about class and inheritance in 19th-century England... and it deals with rural farming life, the role of the church in small communities, and the treatment of servants."

**The Specificity Rule:** Sub topics tend to be *more specific* than main topics. "Rural families" is a sub_topic in a novel whose main_topic is "Class." "The role of the Church of England" is a sub_topic in a novel whose main_topic is "Religion."

---

## Main Topics vs. Sub Topics (Examples)

| Work | Main Topics | Sub Topics |
|---|---|---|
| Wuthering Heights | Interpersonal relations, Class, Inheritance | Cousins, Country life, Rural families, Manners and customs, Landscape |
| The Jungle (Upton Sinclair) | Labor, Capitalism, Immigration | Meatpacking, Factory conditions, Chicago, Poverty wages, Unions |
| Harry Potter | Magic, Education, Good vs. Evil | Quidditch, Wizard banking, Owl post, Herbology |
