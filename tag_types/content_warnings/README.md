# content_warnings

**Definition:** Explicit flags for content that may be distressing, harmful, or inappropriate for certain readers — based on what the work *contains*, not what it is *about*.

---

## What is a Content Warning?

A content warning is a factual flag: this work contains X. It is not a judgment about the work's quality, intent, or artistic merit. A content warning on *Beloved* for slavery and violence does not diminish the novel — it helps the right readers find it at the right time and helps vulnerable readers prepare or avoid.

---

## Core Rules

**Do use content_warnings for:**
- Content that is **present and substantial** in the work — not merely referenced or historically mentioned
- Material that could cause genuine distress or be inappropriate for certain audiences
- Depictions (not just discussions) of the flagged subject

**Do NOT use content_warnings for:**
- Subjects the book is *about* academically or analytically (a history of genocide is a `main_topic`; depictions of genocide in narrative prose are a content warning)
- Mild or commonplace content (conflict, sadness, death by old age do not need warnings)
- Marketing or aesthetic descriptors

**The Presence Rule:** The content must be *depicted*, not just *mentioned*. A novel that references that a character's parent died offscreen does not need a content warning for death. A novel with a graphic, on-page death scene may.

**The Substantiality Rule:** The content must be meaningful in quantity or impact. A single mild reference does not warrant a flag; recurring or significant presence does.

---

## Governance

Content warnings are **strictly controlled**. This is one of the few types where we are conservative about adding new terms:
- Each term has real weight and implications for readers
- Vague or overlapping terms undermine trust in the system
- New terms require maintainer ratification (not just community PR)

Changes require a GitHub Issue with clear definition, distinguishing rules, and at least 5 example works before a PR will be accepted.

---

## Relationship to Other Types

Content warnings are **not** genres, moods, or themes. A book can have a `genres:Horror` tag and a `content_warnings:Graphic violence` tag simultaneously. A `literary_themes:Death` tag does not imply a death content warning — the theme tag indicates meaning, the warning indicates depiction.
