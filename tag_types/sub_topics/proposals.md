# sub_topics — Proposals

`sub_topics` is an open/growing type. This file tracks scope decisions and canonicalization proposals.

See [AGENTS.md](../AGENTS.md) for the distinction between main_topics and sub_topics.

---

## Scope Decisions

### When does something graduate from sub_topic to main_topic?
- **Decision:** 2026-04
- **Rule:** Apply the Centrality Test. Remove the concept from the book — does the central premise collapse? If yes → main_topic. If the book could exist without it (it's background, texture, world-building) → sub_topic.
- **Example:** In Wuthering Heights, *Inheritance and succession* is a main_topic (the estate inheritance drives the plot). *Country life* is a sub_topic (it's the setting's texture, not the engine).

### "Animals" — sub_topic or things?
- **Decision:** 2026-04
- **Rule:** Named, individual animals that characters interact with → `things` (e.g., "Toby the dog"). Categories of animals present in the world but not individuated → `sub_topics` (e.g., "sheep farming" as a sub_topic in a pastoral novel). Animals as a *theme* (the moral status of animals, animal rights) → `literary_themes:Animals` or `main_topics:Animal rights`.
