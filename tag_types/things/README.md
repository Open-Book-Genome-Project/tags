# things

**Definition:** Tangible objects, creatures, or concrete nouns that are physically present and meaningful in the text.

---

## What is a "Thing"?

Things are the **prop list** of the book. If you were staging this story as a play, what objects and creatures would you need to physically have on stage?

Things are present *in the text* — not in the real world, not as themes, not as settings. A book about the history of the printing press has `main_topics` of [Technology, History] but only has `things` if the press itself appears as a physical object that characters interact with.

---

## Core Rules

**Do use things to capture:**
- Physical objects that appear and matter in the story (pistols, silk dresses, broomsticks)
- Creatures and animals that are physically present (dogs, vampires, dragons)
- Significant artifacts, tools, or items characters interact with

**Do NOT put in things:**
- Abstract concepts (those are `literary_themes` or `main_topics`)
- Settings, locations, or environments (those are `places`)
- Characters and people (those are `people`)
- Plot devices that aren't physical objects (those may be `literary_tropes`)

**The Prop Test:** Imagine staging this book as a play. What physical objects would you need to have on stage because characters touch, use, or interact with them? Those are things.

**The Presence Rule:** Things must be *present* in the text, not just referenced historically or metaphorically. A book that discusses guns as a political issue has `main_topics` of [Weapons policy, Second Amendment]. A book in which characters load, fire, and lose pistols has `things` of [Pistols].

**The Specificity Preference:** Be as specific as is meaningful. "Pistols" is better than "weapons." "Broomsticks" is better than "cleaning supplies." But "a wooden object" is too vague to be useful.

## Examples

From Wuthering Heights: Mud, Dogs, Snowstorms, Pistols, Gold, Silk dresses, Whips

From Harry Potter: Broomsticks, Wands, Owls, Potions, The Sorting Hat, The Marauder's Map, Chocolate Frogs

From Dracula: Crucifixes, Garlic, Coffins, Stakes, Mirrors, Wolves, Bats

---

## This is an Open/Growing List

Things are specific to individual works and grow organically. There is no master vocabulary — tags are created per-work as needed.

When tagging:
- Use the common noun (lowercase in application, Title Case in vocabulary files)
- Prefer the specific over the generic when the specific term is clear
- Include creatures and animals alongside inanimate objects
