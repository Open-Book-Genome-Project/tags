# Literary Themes — Vocabulary

An open and growing list. Additions are welcome; see [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md).

Tag format: `theme:term` (e.g., `theme:revenge`)

---

## Core Themes (well-established)

| Tag | Notes |
|---|---|
| Betrayal | Trust broken by someone close; the aftermath of disloyalty |
| Class | Social hierarchy, mobility, and the systems that enforce inequality |
| Coming of Age | Loss of innocence and the formation of adult identity |
| Death | Mortality, grief, loss, the confrontation with finitude |
| Desire | Want, longing, obsession — the gap between what is and what is wanted |
| Duty | Obligation vs. personal will; the cost of loyalty to an external code |
| Fate | Predestination, inevitability, the tension between free will and determinism |
| Freedom | Liberation, escape, the meaning and limits of autonomy |
| Gender | The construction, performance, and constraint of gendered identity |
| Grief | The processing of loss; mourning and its many forms |
| Guilt | The weight of past actions; conscience and moral reckoning |
| Identity | Who we are, how we know, and how others define us |
| Innocence | Purity, naivety, and the forces that corrupt or preserve it |
| Justice | Fairness, retribution, the failure or success of moral and legal systems |
| Love | Romantic, familial, obsessive, sacrificial — the full range of attachment |
| Memory | How the past shapes the present; the reliability and distortion of recollection |
| Mortality | The awareness of death as a shaping force on living |
| Nature | The relationship between humans and the natural world |
| Obsession | Single-minded fixation and its consequences |
| Power | Who has it, who doesn't, and what it does to people |
| Race | Racism, racial identity, and systems of racial power |
| Redemption | The possibility (or impossibility) of making amends or becoming better |
| Rejection | Being cast out, unwanted, or denied belonging |
| Revenge | The drive to repay harm; justice by one's own hand |
| Sacrifice | What we give up — for love, duty, survival, others |
| Survival | The drive to persist in the face of annihilation |
| Truth | Honesty, deception, the elusiveness of objective reality |
| Violence | Its causes, effects, and moral meaning |
| War | Conflict between groups and what it reveals about human nature |

---

*This list is open. Add terms via PR with a brief definition.*
