# Subgenres — Controlled Vocabulary

A managed list. To propose additions or changes, see [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md).

Tag format: `subgenre:term` (e.g., `subgenre:cyberpunk`)

---

| Tag | Definition | Typical Parent Genre(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Apocalyptic | The world is actively ending during the course of the narrative | Horror, Sci-Fi, Fantasy |
| Biopunk | Focuses on biotechnology, genetic engineering, and body modification as the dominant technological framework | Sci-Fi |
| Cli-fi | Climate Fiction; structured around environmental collapse as the defining world-state | Sci-Fi, Drama |
| Coming of Age | Bildungsroman; the structural transition from youth to adult identity as the central arc | Drama, Literary, Romance, Fantasy |
| Cult | Works with transgressive structure and niche or subcultural appeal; often formally experimental | Literary, Horror |
| Cyberpunk | High-tech, low-life; digital networks and urban decay as the defining aesthetic and social framework | Sci-Fi |
| Detective | Structured around the procedural journey of an investigator through a case | Mystery, Crime |
| Dystopian | A world defined by oppressive or failed societal control as the structural baseline | Sci-Fi, Fantasy |
| Epistolary | Narrative constructed entirely from letters, diary entries, documents, or other written records | Drama, Romance, Horror |
| Epic | Massive scope with high political, cosmic, or civilizational stakes; often in a secondary world | Fantasy, Sci-Fi, Historical |
| Espionage | Structured around intelligence operations, state secrets, and institutional betrayal | Thriller, Crime |
| Family Saga | Multi-generational lineage and domestic history as the organizing spine | Drama, Historical |
| Futurism | Grounded, near-future extrapolation focused on the next technological or social steps | Sci-Fi |
| Gonzo | Subjective, chaotic, and author-centric; the narrator's own consciousness shapes the structure | Literary, Humor |
| Gothic | Atmospheric decay, ancestral secrets, the sublime, and dread encoded in setting and architecture | Horror, Romance, Literary |
| Locked Room | A pure logic puzzle; an impossible crime or situation that must be solved by reason alone | Mystery |
| Melodrama | Exaggerated plot and heightened emotional stakes; moral polarity between characters | Drama, Romance |
| Picaresque | Episodic adventures of a roguish, low-status protagonist navigating society's margins | Adventure, Comedy |
| Post-Apocalyptic | Structured around life and society as it rebuilds (or fails to) after a collapse has already occurred | Sci-Fi, Horror, Fantasy |
| Psychological | Internalized action; mental and emotional states drive plot more than external events | Thriller, Horror, Drama |
| Space Opera | Interstellar conflict, galactic politics, and large-scale adventure as the structural scope | Sci-Fi |
| Steampunk | Retrofuturism built on 19th-century steam and mechanical technology as the dominant aesthetic | Sci-Fi, Adventure, Fantasy |
| True Crime | Structural retelling and investigation of real-world criminal events | Crime |
| Utopian | A world structured as an ideal or "perfect" society, often used to critique the present | Sci-Fi, Fantasy |
