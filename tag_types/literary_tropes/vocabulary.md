# Literary Tropes — Vocabulary

An open and growing list. Additions are welcome; see [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md).

Tag format: `trope:term` (e.g., `trope:love-triangle`)

---

## Character Archetypes

| Tag | Definition |
|---|---|
| Antihero | A protagonist who lacks conventional heroic qualities; morally ambiguous or flawed |
| Chosen One | A protagonist prophesied or destined for a special role |
| Femme Fatale | A seductive, dangerous woman whose allure draws others into trouble |
| Foundling | A protagonist of unknown parentage, often with a hidden identity or destiny |
| Mentor | A wise elder who guides the protagonist before departing or dying |
| Orphan Protagonist | The main character has no living parents; their absence shapes the character's arc |
| Trickster | A character who uses cunning, deception, or chaos to achieve goals |
| Unreliable Narrator | The narrator's account is deliberately incomplete, biased, or self-deceiving |
| Villain with a Point | The antagonist's worldview has genuine merit; the conflict is morally complex |
| Wicked Stepparent | A step-parent who is actively hostile or cruel to the protagonist |

## Narrative & Plot Devices

| Tag | Definition |
|---|---|
| Enemies to Lovers | A romantic arc that begins with hostility and transforms into love |
| Epistolary Structure | The narrative is constructed from letters, diary entries, or documents |
| Fake Identity | A character conceals or fabricates their identity for significant portions of the plot |
| False Death | A character appears to die but is later revealed to be alive |
| Found Family | A group of unrelated individuals who form deep familial bonds |
| Forbidden Love | A romantic relationship that is opposed by circumstance, society, or family |
| Frame Narrative | A story-within-a-story; an outer narrative contains the central tale |
| Haunted Past | A character's present is shaped by trauma or secrets from their history |
| In Media Res | The narrative begins in the middle of the action, with backstory revealed later |
| Inheritance and Succession | Property, title, or power passing between generations drives the plot |
| Love Triangle | Three characters in overlapping romantic entanglements |
| MacGuffin | An object or goal that motivates characters but has little intrinsic narrative importance |
| Redemption Arc | A morally compromised character works toward, achieves, or fails at redemption |
| Reverse Chronology | The story is told backwards; the ending is revealed first |
| Rival Turned Ally | A former opponent becomes an ally or friend |
| Slow Burn | A romantic or narrative tension that builds gradually over a long arc |
| The Twist | A major revelation that recontextualizes what the reader understood |
| Time Skip | A significant period of in-story time is elided, skipping forward in the narrative |
