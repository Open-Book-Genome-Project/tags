# Moods — Controlled Vocabulary

A managed list. To propose additions or changes, see [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md).

Tag format: `mood:term` (e.g., `mood:cozy`)

---

| Tag | Definition |
|---|---|
| Calm | Peaceful, unhurried; the reading experience is serene and undemanding |
| Comical | Frequently funny; lightness and humor punctuate the reading experience throughout |
| Confusing | Deliberately disorienting; the reader is meant to feel uncertain or lost |
| Cozy | Warm, safe, intimate; like reading by a fire with a hot drink |
| Dark | Bleak or grim in tone; the world of the book offers little comfort |
| Depressing | The reading experience produces prolonged sadness or hopelessness |
| Disturbing | Unsettling in a way that lingers; challenges the reader's sense of safety or normalcy |
| Exciting | Fast-paced and energetic; the reader feels propelled forward |
| Foreboding | A pervasive sense that something terrible is coming; dread without immediate danger |
| Happy | Joyful and optimistic; the dominant feeling is positive and light |
| Harrowing | Intensely distressing; the reader is put through a difficult emotional experience |
| Humbling | The reading experience produces awe, smallness, or a sense of perspective |
| Inspiring | Motivating and uplifting; the reader feels moved to act or believe |
| Intense | High emotional or narrative pressure sustained throughout |
| Melancholic | A sustained, gentle sadness; wistful rather than hopeless |
| Nostalgic | Evokes longing for a past time, place, or feeling |
| Overwhelming | The scale, emotion, or density of the book is almost too much to contain |
| Playful | Light, inventive, and fun; the author and reader are in a game together |
| Sad | Produces grief or sorrow; distinct from melancholic in its directness |
| Scary | Produces active fear or fright during reading |
| Soapy | Dramatic, juicy, and full of interpersonal entanglements |
| Suspenseful | The reader is held in a state of tension and anticipation |
| Swoonworthy | Produces delight, longing, or a pleasurable romantic ache |
| Thrilling | Produces a rush of excitement or adrenaline |
| Uplifting | Leaves the reader feeling hopeful, moved, or emotionally restored |
| Whimsical | Playfully imaginative and fanciful; gently surreal |
