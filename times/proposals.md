# times — Proposals

`times` is an open/growing type. Tags are created per-work as needed. This file tracks scope and canonicalization decisions.

---

## Scope Decisions

### Named era vs. century
- **Decision:** 2026-04
- **Rule:** Use the most specific named era when one exists and is commonly recognized. `Regency Era` is more informative than `Early 19th Century`. When no recognized named era applies, use the century (`19th Century`) or decade (`1920s`) as appropriate.

### Overlapping eras
- **Decision:** 2026-04
- **Rule:** A work can have multiple `times` tags if it spans multiple eras (a multi-generational saga spanning 1880s–1950s gets both). For works with a primary setting and brief flashbacks, tag the primary era; tag the secondary era only if it's substantial.

### "Contemporary" and "Modern"
- **Decision:** 2026-04
- **Rule:** Avoid the tags "Contemporary" and "Modern" without qualification — they decay as time passes. Instead, use the decade or century. A work set in the present day gets `21st Century` or the specific decade if known (`2010s`, `2020s`). "Contemporary" as a subject tag (contemporary art, contemporary politics) belongs in `main_topics`, not `times`.

### Fictional time periods
- **Decision:** 2026-04
- **Rule:** For Sci-Fi set in the distant future, use `Far Future`. For Fantasy with a clear real-world era analog (feudal Europe-ish settings), use the approximate era with a note that it's fictional (`Medieval Period`, `Bronze Age`). For Fantasy with no real-world analog, omit `times` unless the author specifies.

## Canonicalization Proposals

### Victorian Era
- **Canonical form:** `Victorian Era` (1837–1901; Queen Victoria's reign)
- **Reject on migration:** "Victorian", "Victorian period", "Victorian Age", "19th century England" (if clearly Victorian)

### World War II
- **Canonical form:** `World War II`
- **Reject:** "WWII", "WW2", "Second World War", "the War"

### Regency Era
- **Canonical form:** `Regency Era` (1811–1820 in UK; or broadly, Austen's period ~1800–1830)
- **Note:** Many Romance novels use "Regency" to mean a broader period. Tag as `Regency Era` regardless of exact dates if the author intends the Regency aesthetic.
